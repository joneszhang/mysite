We originally made it for our summer training guided by Oracle. Then we find that there are many points that can be improved so we participated the IBM Mainframe Technology and Skiils Application 2013 National Campus Contest Team Competition. During which we transplanted our server from http to mainframe and optimized our logic. Finally we won the third prize of the competition. For most of us it's the first time to build such a complex project and many techniques were stranger to us, which means we can only learning by doing and to keep high efficiency we had to work more than 12 hours a day. But our passion finally brought us a good result, it was a perfect team work experience.

What's special

As only a group of student, we had no right to access data from banks as Alipay did. So we can only build a virtual bank system by ourselves. We wrote a script to create 20 thousand of records which contains virtual information of name, ID card numbers, bank card numbers and so on. Also, we built our own database to solve the information of our users. All payment operations are solved in server. The data that to be transfered are all encripted by MD5(regarded to be safe at that time) and we add functions like SSO to guarantee the safety of users' accounts.

The design of payment algorithm was also difficult. To decide which card to use, we should compare the balance, special offer of different banks and user's habit. If it concerns about "go Dutch", we need to dynamically calculate the average amount and choose the best card for receiver to eliminate the poundage.

Besides, after we entered the IBM contest, we transplanted our server to a mainframe, which was erected in Dalian and we can only work on it via remote connection. And thanks to the course Introduction of Mainframe, we got a lot of guidiance from our teacher, an engineer with more than 30 years of mainframe based development.

Android!

Before this project, I knew little about android based development, which means quiet large amount of knowlege waits to be absorbed before I started programming. So before our programming work started I downloaded 2GB of learning material and read everyday. Hard as I've tried, my progress of learning still can't meet the demands of the project, so I can only learn by doing. And in only just 3 weeks I learned and applied SQLITE, Sharedpreference and 4 basic tools of andoird like Intent and Broadcast.

Finally I applied the local database with both Sqlite and sharedpreference. Every time users enter the app I'll ask server for updated information, if it failed, I will show the information stored in the local database, which will still help the user. For the UI, I used relative layout, which means my front end could fit most intelligent phones today. Also, I've learned a lot about the application of View library of andoird and implemented a completed front end with visual effects like gallery and 3D Rotation. Also, to contact with the server, I add child threads to solve the transfer work so that the ui could keep smooth when connecting to the server. Also, with the help of intent and broadcast, I implemented the GPS locating and sent shortmessage automatically.
